GeoBotics: Geometric Robot Learning

CoRL 2023 Workshop, Atlanta, GA

Nov 2023, time TBD

Geometry problems in robot perception
Geometric processing for physical environments
SE(3)-equivariant neural networks
Optimization and manifold structure in control
3D generative models for interactions and manipulations
Any topics bridging geometry, learning, and robotics

Geometry is fundamental in enabling robots to navigate complex environments, manipulate objects precisely, and seamlessly interact with humans. By harnessing geometric representations, algorithms, and deep learning techniques, robots can extract and reason about underlying geometric structures and relationships in their sensory data. In this workshop, discussions will revolve around the importance of geometry in robot learning, the interplay between deep learning and geometric reasoning, and the potential for further advancements. Relevant topics include but are not limited to:

  • Geometry problems in robot perception
  • Geometric processing for physical environments
  • SE(3) equivariant neural networks
  • Optimization and manifold structure in control
  • 3D generative models for interaction and manipulation
  • Any topics bridging geometry, learning, and robotics
